E veryone in George’s family is *so* embarrassing. It is a sentiment many of us remember from childhood, and one that Kimber Fox Morgan captures perfectly in her delightful book, Embarrassing George . In honor of National Authors Day, we are stepping away from the stove to celebrate the stories that bring families together—quirks and all.

THE ORIGINAL COVER ART FOR EMBARRASSING GEORGE BY KIMBER FOX MORGAN

George's world is a whirlwind of relatability: his mom can’t cook, his sister is constantly making kissy faces to her phone, and his brother’s wardrobe consists of nothing but costumes. To top it off, George’s dad is known for going outdoors in his underwear! It leads to the ultimate childhood dread: Will Family Night at school reveal all of their quirks, or will George want to hide under his desk to avoid them?

A Tale of Family Quirks

COZY CHILDREN'S READING NOOK

Kimber’s storytelling reminds us that the "Heart of the Table" isn't just about the food—it’s about the people gathered around it, even the embarrassing ones. Whether it's a "Family Night" at school or a Tuesday night dinner, these quirks are the threads that sew the tapestry of our legacy. Celebrating National Authors Day gives us a chance to recognize the creators in our own families who document these "embarrassing" but precious moments.

If you have a young reader in your life (or if you simply appreciate a story about a dad in his underwear), George’s journey to self-acceptance is a beautiful addition to your home library.
