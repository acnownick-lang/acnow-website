INSTALLATION FAQ

Frequently Asked AC Installation Questions

Find expert answers to common questions regarding AC replacements, high-efficiency SEER2 standards, warranties, and local building permits.

What is SEER2, and what is the minimum rating required in Florida?

SEER2 (Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio 2) measures an air conditioner’s cooling efficiency. Under Florida building codes, all residential split system air conditioners installed must have a **minimum rating of 14.3 SEER2**. Upgrading to higher SEER2 systems lowers monthly electric bills and qualifies for federal tax credits.

What warranties are included with a new AC installation?

A/C Now LLC includes a **10-year worry-free equipment warranty** on all major residential systems we install. This covers replacement parts directly through the manufacturer. Additionally, we provide a **1-year to 2-year labor warranty** on our work, ensuring your new unit is fully protected.

Does A/C Now LLC handle the building permit process?

Yes. We handle the entire local municipal permitting process for your new AC installation. The state of Florida requires building permits and inspections for all HVAC changeouts to ensure safety, structural wind load limits, and code compliance. We file permits in Port St. Lucie, Stuart, Palm City, and Jupiter, and coordinate inspections.
