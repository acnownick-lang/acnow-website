THE HEART OF THE TABLE

Foodie Friends on Buford Highway

I’m embarking on a new year of writing that celebrates others like me, who LIVE TO EAT! Just such a person is 28-year-old Alex Rold.

A 7-MILE PILGRIMAGE

Rold takes eye-popping, UNREAL, mouthwatering photos of all his foodie adventures in Atlanta, Georgia. It’s not the marketing analyst’s day job but perhaps it should be. I know his mother… yet I found my food-centric cohort purely through drool of mouth. Suffice it to say, I was hardly the first one to notice Alex’s keen eye for superlative places to eat. He was featured on television a little over a year ago, when he said his nosh hobby really took hold and sent him on a photographic tour of the famed Buford Highway.

DEEP SZECHUAN FLAVORS ON THE HIGHWAY

The Bright Lights of the Market

FLUFFY JAPANESE PANCAKES!

“If you’re not used to Buford, it can be overwhelming,” Rold told me. “The diversity you’ll find there... it’s the best Asian food, best true Szechuan food, I’ve had in my life. I’m there almost every night of the week.” He recommended we all try the numbing peppers at Good Luck Gourmet and Masterpiece.

“I recognize friends from the internet on Buford Highway all the time—people I never would have met were it not for our shared love of eating,” he said. A quick glance at his community yields recommendations for the best fried Korean chicken wings to massive Japanese pancakes. I urge you to branch out and explore your own local highway—you never know who you will meet across the table.
