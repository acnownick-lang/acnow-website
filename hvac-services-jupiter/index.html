SEASONAL RESIDENT SPECIALTY

The Snowbird Home Watch AC Guide

Leaving your Florida home vacant for the hot, humid summer months carries significant risk if your HVAC system fails. Elevated indoor relative humidity (above 60%) quickly fosters mold growth, potentially causing thousands of dollars in property damage.

Our specialty **Snowbird Pre-Departure Inspection** includes clearing condensate lines to prevent blockages, setting programmable thermostats to vacant humidity levels (typically 78°F or humidistat settings), and installing secondary safety float switches. We also coordinate with local home-watch services to verify system status, ensuring total peace of mind while you are away.

Clearance Compliance Guide

18"+
Side Clearance Limits: Keep decorative fences and plants at least 18 to 24 inches away from condenser sides to prevent heat buildup recirculations.
5 FT
Overhead Clearance Limits: Ensure at least 5 feet of clear space directly above the outdoor fan grille. Never block top discharge air with patio roofs or deck overhangs.
CODE
Electrical Disconnect Access: Municipal codes require a 36-inch clear path in front of the electrical switchbox. Include a gate panel or removable screen slats.

How often should I service my AC?

In Florida's hot, humid climate, HVAC systems should be professionally serviced at least twice per year — once before the summer cooling season (spring) and once before the winter heating season (fall).
