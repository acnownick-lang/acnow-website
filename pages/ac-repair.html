COMMON CAUSES

What's Wrong with My AC?

Failed Capacitor

If your outdoor fan hums but doesn't spin, the start/run capacitor has likely failed due to high operating heat limits.

Low Refrigerant

If your lineset is freezing or the unit runs non-stop without cooling, a refrigerant leak is likely choking the system flow.

Clogged Drain Line

Condensate lines clog with algae over time, triggering the emergency float safety switch to shut down all system power.

What is HVAC repair?

HVAC repair is the professional diagnosis and restoration of a heating, ventilation, and air conditioning system by licensed technicians. It includes identifying failed components such as capacitors, compressors, refrigerant leaks, or blower motors, then repairing or replacing them to restore full cooling and heating performance. How often should I service my AC?

In Florida's hot, humid climate, HVAC systems should be professionally serviced at least twice per year — once before the summer cooling season (spring) and once before the winter heating season (fall). Twice-yearly tune-ups prevent breakdowns, extend system life, and maintain manufacturer warranties.
